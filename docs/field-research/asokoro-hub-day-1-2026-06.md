# Asokoro Tanker Hub — Field Visit, Day 1 (June 2026)

## Questions prepared

**1. Demand / Orders**
- How do customers usually find you? Calls? Agents? WhatsApp? Referral?
- Which areas do orders come from? Maitama? Gwarinpa? Lugbe?
- Which time does demand peak? Morning? Evening? Dry season?
- How many jobs can one tanker do per day?

**2. Pricing / Economics**
- How much do you charge from here to different areas?
- What determines price? Distance? Fuel? Bad road? Queue?
- If someone needs half a tanker or a small amount, do you do that?
- How much fuel does one trip burn roughly?
- Which hurts most right now — diesel, maintenance, or lack of customers?

**3. Operational Pain**
- What's the biggest problem in the tanker business right now?
- Do customers ever waste your time after booking?
- Do customers owe money? Do people price and disappear?
- How long do you wait before the next job?
- If many tankers are idle, how do you share jobs?

**4. Trust / Safety**
- How does the customer know the tanker is full?
- Do customers complain that the water wasn't complete?
- Do some customers fear fraud?
- How do you prove delivery happened?

**5. Tech / App Questions**
- If an app could bring real customers directly, would you use it?
- If your phone rang with nearby jobs, would you accept quickly?
- Do you prefer call, WhatsApp, or app notification?
- Do many drivers here have Android phones?
- Who normally owns the tanker — driver or owner ("oga")?
- If the owner is elsewhere, who decides which jobs to take?

**6. Batch Delivery (strategic question — listen carefully)**
- If 3 people in the same area need small amounts of water at the same time, can one tanker deliver to all of them?
- Would that save fuel?
- Would drivers like that or hate it?

## What happened / answers

Visited the tanker hub in Asokoro. While talking to two of the tankers, their leader came around — he mentioned someone else (described as dark, tall, with a beard) had already visited some days earlier doing similar research. He agreed to give his attention to the questions.

1. **How customers find them:** Truecaller, sometimes WhatsApp.
2. **Where orders come from:** Mostly Asokoro, also Maitama.
3. **Demand timing:** Highest during dry season (roughly January–March). Currently (rainy season) demand is low.
4. **Jobs per day:** Up to 10–11, depending.
5. **Pricing:** Up to ₦70–80k per tanker. Some runs go for ₦35–40k, others ₦40k/₦50k/₦60k. Diesel was ₦2,300/liter, now down to ₦1,900/liter.
6. **What determines price:** Distance, then fuel price. Also tank elevation (e.g. a tank on a tank stand), bad roads, and queueing.
7. **Partial payment:** They don't do partial/installment for small tankers — the customer pays in full upfront.
8. **Fuel consumption:** 30L of diesel covers roughly 3–4 trips, plus about 1.5L of fuel for the pumping machine per trip.
9. **Biggest pain:** Maintenance — a single incident can cost up to ₦200k.

The leader cut the conversation short here (said to continue another time and left to rest) before reaching the trust/safety and tech questions — those were picked up on Day 2 instead. His number was collected, and 2 crates of soda were left with one of his boys as a thank-you gesture.
