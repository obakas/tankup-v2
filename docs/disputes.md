# Dispute Management System

## Purpose

The Dispute Management System exists to handle:

# operational disagreement.

In real-world logistics,
conflict is inevitable.

The purpose is NOT to eliminate all disputes.

The purpose is to:

- resolve disagreements fairly,
- preserve operational trust,
- protect all actors,
- reduce repeated abuse,
- maintain platform credibility.

---

# Core Philosophy

Disputes are operational signals.

A dispute may reveal:

- operational weakness,
- fraud,
- communication failure,
- pricing mismatch,
- delivery proof weakness,
- behavioral problems.

Disputes should generate:

# operational learning,
not only punishment.

---

# Major Dispute Actors

## Customer

May raise complaints regarding:

- delivery quality,
- delivery quantity,
- driver behavior,
- delays,
- operational failure.

---

## Driver

May report:

- customer aggression,
- accessibility deception,
- unsafe conditions,
- refusal to cooperate,
- fake dispute claims.

---

## Fleet Head

May coordinate:

- operational clarification,
- driver defense,
- payout disagreements,
- incident escalation.

---

## Admin

Acts as:

# operational arbitrator.

Admins govern disputes,
not emotions.

---

# Behavioral Disputes

# Damage Disputes

# Fraud Disputes

---

# Dispute Lifecycle

Disputes move through operational stages.

---

# Stage 1 — Raised

Dispute officially submitted.

---

# Required Inputs

Dispute should contain:

- dispute type,
- description,
- related delivery,
- supporting evidence optional,
- timestamps.

---

# Stage 2 — Review

Platform evaluates available operational evidence.

---

# Evidence Sources

Review may consider:

- delivery records,
- OTP logs,
- timestamps,
- site intelligence,
- photos,
- incident reports,
- fleet reports,
- historical behavior.

---

# Stage 3 — Investigation

Additional operational clarification may occur.

---

# Investigation Actions

Examples:

- request additional evidence
- contact fleet head
- contact customer
- review operational logs
- verify status transitions

---

# Important Principle

Admins should investigate:

# operational facts,
not emotional narratives.

---

# Stage 4 — Resolution

Dispute receives formal decision.

---

# Possible Outcomes

## customer_favored

Customer claim validated.

---

## driver_favored

Driver/fleet operational position validated.

---

## partial_resolution

Shared operational responsibility identified.

---

## insufficient_evidence

No reliable conclusion possible.

---

## escalated

Requires higher operational review.

---

# Stage 5 — Closure

Dispute finalized.

Operational consequences may apply.

---

# Evidence Philosophy

Evidence should be:

- structured,
- timestamped,
- operationally relevant.

---

# Strong Evidence Examples

- OTP logs
- arrival timestamps
- measurement records
- photos
- sequential status integrity

---

# Weak Evidence Examples

- unsupported accusations
- emotional claims
- unverifiable assumptions

---

# Important Operational Insight

The platform should prioritize:

# operational consistency over storytelling.

Consistent operational records become powerful evidence.

---

# Delivery Proof Integration

Dispute resolution heavily depends on:

# Delivery Proof System.

Weak delivery proof creates:

- payout instability,
- operational distrust,
- refund chaos.

---

# Site Intelligence Integration

Repeated disputes influence:

# Site Intelligence.

---

# Example Effects

A site with repeated operational conflict may receive:

- higher risk score,
- verification requirement,
- reduced batching priority.

---

# Behavioral Trust Scores

Disputes influence operational trust.

---

# Customer Trust Impact

Repeated harmful behavior may reduce:

- customer trust score,
- dispatch priority,
- batching eligibility.

---

# Driver/Fleet Trust Impact

Repeated operational negligence may reduce:

- assignment priority,
- operational reputation,
- dispatch preference.

---

# Refund Logic Integration

Some disputes may trigger:

- full refund,
- partial refund,
- payout hold,
- operational compensation.

---

# Important Operational Rule

Refunds should NOT become automatic emotional reactions.

Operational evidence must guide outcomes.

---

# Payout Hold Integration

High-severity disputes may temporarily freeze payouts.

---

# Hold Goals

Prevent:

- irreversible payout mistakes,
- fraud exploitation,
- operational injustice.

---

# False Dispute Protection

The platform must protect against:

# weaponized disputes.

---

# Examples

Customers may attempt:

- fake under-delivery claims
- false aggression accusations
- refund abuse patterns

Drivers/fleets may attempt:

- fake completion
- evidence manipulation
- operational concealment

---

# Repeat Abuse Handling

Repeated abuse patterns may trigger:

- account restriction,
- manual verification,
- operational suspension,
- platform ban future.

---

# Fleet Head Role

Fleet heads may serve as:

# operational stabilizers.

They may help:

- calm conflict,
- verify operational reality,
- coordinate recovery,
- mediate misunderstandings.

---

# Admin Governance Principles

Admins should remain:

- evidence-driven,
- operationally neutral,
- audit-conscious.

---

# Important Governance Rule

All dispute actions must generate:

# audit logs.

No invisible resolution changes.

---

# Dispute Severity Levels

---

# Low Severity

Minor operational disagreement.

Examples:

- mild lateness
- communication issue

---

# Medium Severity

Operational review required.

Examples:

- accessibility disagreement
- moderate delivery complaint

---

# High Severity

Major operational conflict.

Examples:

- repeated false reporting
- severe operational negligence
- aggressive confrontation

---

# Critical Severity

Immediate intervention required.

Examples:

- physical threat
- severe fraud
- dangerous operational behavior

---

# Long-Term Strategic Goal

Over time,
TankUp should become capable of identifying:

- likely dispute patterns,
- high-conflict customers,
- operationally unstable sites,
- fraud behavior trends.

Dispute intelligence becomes:

# operational governance intelligence.

That governance layer becomes one of the strongest trust advantages of the platform.
